A polyhedric artist

Here's some of Woodkid's career cornerstones:

  • 2004 - He moves from Lyon, where he graduated summa cum laude at the Emile Cohl School, to Paris to work for H5
  • 2006 - He works with Luc Besson for the movie Arthur and the Invisibles and directs a series of roughs for Sofia Coppola's Marie Antoinette
  • 2010 - One of his short movies, Graffiti, wins five Lions at Cannes Lions International Festival of Creativity
  • 2011 - The single Iron gets released and becomes an instant succes, putting him under the spotlight
  • 2013 - The Golden Age, Woodkid releases his first album, which is soon praised by critics and public. That same year, during The Golden Age Tour he performs with BBC's Orchestra at the London Brixton Acadamy
  • 2015 - Jonás Cuarón asks him to compose the soundtrack for his latest movie, Desierto
  • 2020 - On December 2019 Woodkid announces he's been working on a new album, and, on october 2020, he releases S16, a very introspective album that pushes forward Woodkid's own artistic boundaries
  • 2022 - The World Tour for S16, stopped during 2021 due to the pandemic crisis, restarts from Italy
